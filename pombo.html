Predicting Classroom Inefficacies

Software and Data at Pombo Education

In the midst of the pandemic, my friends and I co founded a non-profit that aims to provide a data-based platform for colombian teachers in low income schools to monitor their students’ achievement based on the results of their own teaching style. The app includes sample worksheets, activities, tests, suggested benchmarks, surveys, teacher chat rooms, teaching tips and blog posts. The teachers are able to gauge the effectiveness of their teaching methods through the results of analytical and standardized surveys given to the students on a regular basis.

The students get surveys after each unit so they can give feedback to their teachers. This way, teachers can evaluate what’s working and what isn’t and adjust their teaching style/methods to the students’ needs. Gathering data of how students respond to different teaching methods all over the country could really help understand how to improve education in Colombia. We want to gather the data to research about what is actually going on in Colombian classrooms, and how to use these data to improve the quality of education by sharing with policy makers, schools, and research institutions, in hopes of then expanding to other latinamerican countries.

My contributions were the creation of the app in Swift, firebase infrastructure, the chatbot assistant, and statistical models from gathered data.
