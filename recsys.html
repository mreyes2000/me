Recommender Systems Applied to Economic Diversification

Research at Harvard Growth Lab

I am currently working at the Harvard Growth Lab with Dr. Andrés Gómez-Liévano developing and implementing recommender system models to recommend to developing countries the products that they should export in order to maximize their economic indices such as their GDP. We have tried implementing various algorithms including content-based approaches with nearest-neighbor, regression, and classification algorithms. We have also implemented collaborative filtering memory and model-based methods with matrix factorization algorithms. We are currently tackling the challenge of accounting for time. It seems like the variance and mean of the values in our items times product matrix for each year grow logarithmically as a function of time, so we are using Yehuda Koren’s research to take a time-series based approach. We are also evaluating if making Markovian assumptions make a good model, and finally coming up with a time-dependent cross-validation algorithm to train our model progressively as time increases, training on n years and testing on n+1 years worth of data.
